In the aftermath, Marcus Antonius seized the opportunity to address the grieving citizens. Standing atop the Rostra in the Forum, he delivered a stirring funeral oration that turned the tide of public opinion. With calculated words, he painted Caesar as a fallen hero, a martyr for the cause of a united Rome.

The citizens, swayed by Antonius's impassioned speech, turned against the conspirators. Civil unrest gripped the streets as the fragile peace of Rome shattered. The Senate, once a bastion of order, now faced the wrath of a divided people.

The story of Julius Caesar's account unfolded like a tragic play, with power, ambition, and betrayal at its core. The repercussions of that fateful day echoed through history, shaping the course of Rome and leaving an indelible mark on the annals of time.
